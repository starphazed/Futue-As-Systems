A Simulation of Data and Power

In an age where data is the new currency, who controls your personal information? Explore the complex relationship between government surveillance, corporate data collection, and individual privacy in this interactive simulation.

The privacy paradox refers to the tension between the desire for personal privacy and the need to share personal data for convenience, safety, or economic benefits. In our modern world, we willingly trade privacy for services, but the implications of this trade are often unknown.

In this simulation, you can adjust privacy settings for different groups (government, corporations, and individuals). The power distribution between these groups will shift based on your choices, and you’ll see how it impacts society, economy, and privacy.

How Does It Work?

The simulation allows you to make critical decisions about data privacy in a futuristic society. You will be able to control how much information is shared with governments, corporations, and individuals. As you make these decisions, watch how the balance of power shifts between these groups, affecting the world’s economy, social structure, and the well-being of its citizens.

What Will You Choose?

Will you empower the government to collect more data in the name of security? Or will you protect the privacy of individuals, even at the cost of economic efficiency? Your choices will have real consequences, affecting everything from healthcare access to corporate dominance in the market.

Why Does Privacy Matter?

As governments and corporations increase their reach into our personal lives, it’s more important than ever to understand the trade-offs we make for convenience. Do we give up some privacy for personalized services? How do we protect vulnerable groups from being exploited by mass data collection? This simulation asks you to think critically about these questions and consider the consequences of your actions.
